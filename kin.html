1. Focus marketing spend by tracking ROI

There are four ways a visitor can arrive at the Kin Euphorics website, and I’ll work with your marketing team to track their efforts for each path by: developing a structured process to embed UTM parameters in every link on the web, and implementing event-specific landing pages and discount codes to track field-marketing impact.

Tracking how a customer is first captured into the lifecycle is critical for spending the marketing budget in the channels that lead to the highest conversions, or lead to the highest number of product/brand promoters.

2. Increase engagement by segmenting website visitors

Segmentation on the website is key. We want to be able to do things like: giving “hot” users a fast lane to make a purchase; identifying “warm” users and focusing them on indirect sales channels; and educating “cold” users so they have enough information to tell friends about it even though they may never buy anything.
‍
I’ll work with you and your engineering team to define the rules for segmenting website visitors by their behaviour and engagement. Then, I will analyze that along with purchase patterns to implement those rules using the tools you’re already using, like Klaviyo, Hotjar, and Privy.

Segmenting website traffic by behaviour enables a more targeted approach to serving content most relevant to the visitor.

3. Increase Direct and Indirect Conversions

Once we have segmentation, we can work on improving targeting to increase conversions. Visitors browsing cocktail recipes might be more interested in High Rhode, while visitors from Instagram might prefer Spritz. Visitors who don't want to buy now but are interested in trying Kin could be directed to our indirect-sales partners like west~bourne, or sent an invite to a Kin-event.
‍
To make these a reality and more, I’ll work with you to understand the overarching business goals, think about how to optimize the website and various conversion channels to achieve those goals, and work hand-in-hand with the marketing and engineering teams to implement any data collection that might be needed.

Promoting indirect sales channels could be just as important to increase sales long-term.

4. Encouraging post-purchase actions and driving loyalty.

A first-mover product like Kin is going to generate some buzz. The impact of word of mouth can be difficult to capture, but we can leverage tools you’re already using like Yotpo to give net promoters small discounts for their friends to purchase. This would allow us to track the people who are truly excited about the product, and give them some ammo to spread the word. I can help develop ideas on how to best optimize this stage of the customer lifecycle, and own the implementation from start to finish.

We can build a steady revenue base by encouraging repeat-purchases and word-of-mouth marketing in a targeted manner.

Two Easy Wins to Increase Conversions

As promised, here are the two improvements that I think will immediately increase conversions:
‍
1. Change the colour of the splash button: Make the conversion button in the splash section on the landing page stand out more. Having the white button on a light background makes it hard to see - I tested it with three friends who are digital natives between the ages of 20-25, and none of them saw the button.

2. Add an email form to notify when back in stock: The High Rhode product is currently sold out, but if the user is only interested in that product there’s no way for you to capture that demand. Adding a small email form to “notify me when this is back in stock” should help capture some of that demand.
